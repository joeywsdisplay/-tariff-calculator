US Tariff Policy Timeline — 2025–2026

Key legislative and executive actions that shape the current tariff landscape for aluminum and steel imports.

Jan 2025
Baseline MFN tariffs in effect
Standard Most Favored Nation (MFN) rates apply. Effective tariff rate on all imports: 2.3%. Section 301 tariffs (7.5%–100%) on Chinese goods from Trump's first term remain active.
Mar 4, 2025
IEEPA fentanyl tariffs — Canada & Mexico
25% tariffs imposed on Canadian and Mexican imports via IEEPA authority, citing fentanyl emergency. USMCA-compliant goods paused/exempted for qualifying shipments.
Mar 12, 2025
Section 232 steel & aluminum doubled to 25%
Proclamations 10895 & 10896 raise Section 232 steel and aluminum tariffs from prior rates to 25% on all imports, with no country exemptions remaining.
Apr 2, 2025
"Liberation Day" — IEEPA reciprocal tariffs announced
Executive order announces country-specific reciprocal tariffs for 60+ nations ranging from 10% to 49% (China eventually reaching 145%). Described as the largest unilateral tariff action since Smoot-Hawley (1930).
Apr 9, 2025
90-day pause — most IEEPA rates rolled back to 10%
All country-specific rates above 10% are paused for 90 days to allow negotiations. Baseline 10% IEEPA tariff takes effect for most countries. China excluded — rates continue escalating.
May 8, 2025
US–UK bilateral framework deal
Framework agreement reduces UK auto tariffs and provides steel/aluminum quota arrangements. Baseline 10% maintained. UK later receives 25% (vs 50%) rate on steel/aluminum under Proclamation 11021.
May 12, 2025
US–China Geneva truce
US reduces IEEPA tariff on China from 125% to 10% for 90 days. China reduces retaliatory tariffs from 125% to 10%. Section 301 tariffs and 20% fentanyl IEEPA tariff remain.
Jun 4, 2025
Section 232 steel & aluminum doubled again — to 50%
Steel and aluminum Section 232 tariffs double from 25% to 50% for all countries. UK receives reduced rate. Australia retains bilateral exemption.
Aug 18, 2025
407 new HTS codes added to Section 232 derivative scope
Bureau of Industry and Security adds 407 HTSUS codes as Section 232 steel/aluminum derivative products — expanding coverage to Chapters 83, 84, 85, 87, and 94, including lamps and lighting fittings.
Nov 7, 2025
US–China truce extended one year
China's reduced 10% IEEPA rate extended through November 10, 2026. Section 301 tariffs and Section 232 remain fully in force on Chinese goods.
Feb 20, 2026
Supreme Court strikes down all IEEPA tariffs
Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump — Supreme Court rules all IEEPA-based tariffs unconstitutional. ~$130–160B in collected duties subject to potential refund via Court of International Trade.
Feb 24, 2026
Section 122 global tariff takes effect
10% temporary import surcharge on all countries under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974. Expires automatically ~July 24, 2026 without Congressional extension. Exempt: Sec. 232 goods, USMCA-compliant goods.
Apr 2, 2026
Proclamation 11021 — Section 232 restructured
Major restructuring of Section 232 framework. Tariffs now applied to full customs value (not just metal content). New tiered structure: 50% for primary metals, 25% for substantially metal derivatives, 15% combined minimum for metal-intensive industrial goods through Dec 2027.
May 2026
Current regime — as of this calculator
Section 232 (50%/25%/15% tiered) + Section 301 on China + Section 122 (10%, expiring ~Jul 24) + MFN base duties. Congressional action pending on replacement tariff authority. Refund proceedings ongoing at Court of International Trade.
Sources: White House proclamations, Congressional Research Service, Yale Budget Lab, Tax Foundation Tariff Tracker, HSRates.com, US CBP. Updated May 2026.
